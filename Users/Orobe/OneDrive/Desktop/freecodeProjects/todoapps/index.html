What is Bitcoin Halving?

The Bitcoin Halving is a pre-programmed event that reduces the reward given to crypto miners by half approximately every four years. The Halving is essential because it ensures that the supply of Bitcoin is kept in check and prevents inflation. The reduction of mining rewards means that the demand for Bitcoin is expected to increase, and its value is likely to surge in response. It is anticipated to happen every 210,000 blocks or roughly every four years, with the most recent halving occurred in May 2020, reducing the reward from 12.5 to 6.25 BTC per block. The Bitcoin Halving plays a crucial role in the long-term viability and scarcity of Bitcoin.

A Look Back at Bitcoin Halvings

November 28th, 2012: The First Slice

BTC Price: $12.20

The first halving sliced off the first 50% of the Bitcoin mining reward â€“ from 50 to 25 Bitcoins per block. Back then, the impact of the halving was still not priced in. The community was watching anxiously, as the closing price on the halving day was ($12.20). In the months after the first halving in 2012, the network hash rate and difficulty declined as less profitable miners turned off machines to reduce costs.

However, by early 2013, Bitcoin kicked off its first major bull run, surging from $13 to over $1,000 by year's end. The fears of miner capitulations were proven largely unfounded. The network hummed along, and the Bitcoin community learned an early lesson: Halvings are bullish for the Bitcoin network and the price of Bitcoin.

July 9th, 2016: The Block Reward Halved Again

BTC Price: $657.57

The second halving took place on July 9th, 2016. The reward for mining a block was cut in half, from 25 to 12.5 BTC. Following the halving, Bitcoin's price saw a gradual increase over the next year, eventually reaching new all-time highs in December 2017.

May 11th, 2020: Halving Amidst a Global Pandemic

BTC Price: $8,721.76

The third Bitcoin halving occurred on May 11th, 2020, amidst the COVID-19 pandemic. The block reward was reduced from 12.5 to 6.25 BTC. Despite initial concerns about the pandemic's impact on Bitcoin's price, the halving event was followed by a significant rally in the cryptocurrency market.

2024: Upcoming Halving

The next Bitcoin halving is scheduled to occur in 2024. As with previous halvings, there is anticipation and speculation about its potential impact on the price and the overall Bitcoin ecosystem.
